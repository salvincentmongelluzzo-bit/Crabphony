Crabfiny is a neon-infused browser idle game where you manage a vibrant aquatic ecosystem centered on breeding increasingly valuable crabs. The core loop involves populating a tank with base-level crabs—like Red and Blue—and feeding them to generate points. As the tank thrives, you use those points to upgrade food quality and tank capacity, shifting the experience from active clicking to a sleek, passive management simulation that runs smoothly in a Chrome tab.

The depth of the game lies in the genetic breeding tree and color-coded tier system. While standard Red, Blue, and Green crabs provide steady income, the real goal is to discover rare mutations like Pink and Orange through strategic feeding and cross-breeding. This adds a discovery element where you strive to unlock every entry in a "Crab-pedia," turning a simple point-generator into a quest for rare biological traits where higher-tier crabs offer exponential point multipliers.

To ensure long-term replayability, Sal, the game uses a "Carcinization" prestige mechanic. Once the current tank reaches its peak efficiency, you can trigger a global reset, evolving the entire operation into a more advanced state. This reset rewards you with permanent "DNA Points" used to buy meta-upgrades—such as increased mutation rates or automated hatchery systems—ensuring that each subsequent "run" starts faster and reaches higher tiers than the one before it.

Management becomes more complex as you unlock specialized environments like Deep Sea Vents or Coral Reefs. Each habitat introduces new variables like water temperature and salinity that must be optimized using automated sensors and regulators. This layer of strategy forces you to balance resources between buying more crabs and upgrading the life-support systems required to keep the most exotic, high-earning species alive while the game runs in the background.

From a technical standpoint, the game is designed to be lightweight and persistent. Using local storage, the game tracks the exact timestamp when the browser tab is closed, allowing the engine to calculate "offline gains" the moment you return. This ensures the crabs are always working and breeding, providing a satisfying "big number" jump after a few hours away, making it a low-friction experience that rewards consistent check-ins without requiring constant attention.
